Foundation

"Foundation" by Isaac Asimov is a classic science fiction novel and the first book in the Foundation series. It is set in a future galactic empire and follows the mathematician Hari Seldon, who has developed a new field of mathematics known as "psychohistory" that can predict the future on a large scale. Seldon predicts the fall of the Galactic Empire and the subsequent dark age that will last for thousands of years. To shorten this period of chaos, he establishes a foundation of knowledge on a remote planet called Terminus. The novel spans centuries and explores the rise and fall of civilizations, the power of science and technology, and the enduring quest for knowledge and survival. Isaac Asimov's "Foundation" is a masterpiece of science fiction and a seminal work in the genre. It continues to captivate readers with its epic scope and timeless themes.

About the Author

Isaac Asimov was a prolific American author, professor, and biochemist, best known for his works of science fiction and popular science writing. He wrote or edited over 500 books and is considered one of the "Big Three" science fiction writers, along with Arthur C. Clarke and Robert A. Heinlein.

Asimov's writing spanned a wide range of subjects, from hard science fiction to mysteries and history. His ability to convey complex scientific concepts in an accessible manner made him a beloved figure in both the literary and scientific communities.
