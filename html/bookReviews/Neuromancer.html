Neuromancer

"Neuromancer" by William Gibson is a groundbreaking science fiction novel that introduced the world to the cyberpunk genre. Set in a dystopian future, the story follows Case, a washed-up computer hacker, who is hired by a mysterious employer for one last job. This job takes Case into the world of cyberspace, a virtual reality realm where he must navigate corporate intrigue, artificial intelligence, and a cast of memorable characters. The novel explores themes of technology, artificial intelligence, and the blurred line between humanity and machines. "Neuromancer" is known for its visionary and gritty depiction of a future where computer technology and cybernetics dominate every aspect of life. It has had a profound influence on science fiction and remains a must-read for fans of the genre.

About the Author

William Gibson is an American-Canadian science fiction author known for his contributions to the cyberpunk genre. He is credited with coining the term "cyberspace" and is considered one of the genre's pioneers.

Gibson's works often explore the impact of technology on society and individuals, and his writing is characterized by its noir style and futuristic visions. "Neuromancer" is his most famous and influential work.
